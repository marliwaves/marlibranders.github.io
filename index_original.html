We Are Different

I am 17 years old and live in Cape Town, South Africa.
From the outside my life might look ordinary,
some may even say uninspiring.
Loving family, exceptional school and almost no difficulties at all.
But from the start I knew I was different. I can see things that others are apparently missing. I can see the hurt and the pain in this world. And I can see that very few are doing anything about it.
Some say it is a curse

Others call it a blessing. But I believe compassion is a vital part of our human structure that many live without. If selfishness was classified as a sickness our world would be fatally ill.
What are the symptoms you ask?

According to Global Issues the poorest 40 percent of the world’s population accounts for 5 percent of global income while the wealthiest 20 percent accounts for three-quarters of world income.
If that does not indicate an epidemic, I do not know what will
Along my life journey I have met many who can see as well. Family, friends, teachers – I know there are people out there that strive every day to make the world a better place. And they are the reason I have hope. They are the reason I believe in a brighter future. They are the reason I have decided that I want to make a difference.
All my efforts would be in vain

without help from above and therefore I have prayed and asked Father God for guidance as I take my first step of action. I firmly believe He has lead me to attend a Discipleship Training School at YWAM Yosemite.
Here I will be able to expand my skills of ministry as well as make a difference through outreach. Unfortunately this endeavour will cost money and therefore I am asking all my fellow see-ers out there to help me make a difference by donating any amount possible.
